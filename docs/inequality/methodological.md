<details class="question-accordion">

<summary>
<span class="dimension">Objects of Analysis</span>
<span class="question">
Which <i>aspects of social inequality</i> are constituted as objects of analysis?
</span>
</summary>

<div class="accordion-body">

<div class="challenge-grid">

<div>

<h3>Methodological Challenges</h3>

<ul>
<li>
High heterogeneity of dimensions and manifestations of social inequality challenges the development of appropriate methodologies.<sup class="citation">1</sup>
</li>
</ul>

</div>

<div>

<h3>Potential Approaches</h3>

<ul>
<li>Economic inequality.</li>
<li>Educational inequality.</li>
<li>Unequal social participation.</li>
<li>Unequal distribution of health and illness.</li>
</ul>

</div>

</div>

</div>

</details>


<details class="question-accordion">

<summary>
<span class="dimension">Mechanisms of Inequality</span>
<span class="question">
How are the <i>mechanisms</i> that (re-)produce and structure social inequality conceptualized, theoretically framed, and empirically analysed?
</span>
</summary>

<div class="accordion-body">

<div class="challenge-grid">

<div>

<h3>Methodological Challenges</h3>

<ul>

<li>
Descriptive approaches (see <a href="/relationality/methodological">'addition'</a>) leave mechanisms in a "black box", <br>allowing alternative, <u>non-power-sensitive explanations</u>. <sup class="citation">2,3,4</sup>
</li>

<b>
Biologization, especially in health research. 
</b>

<b>
Meritocratic logics, especially in educational research. 
</b>

<b>
Behavior-focused explanations that ignore structural inequalities.
</b>

</ul>

</div>

<div>

<h3>Potential Approaches</h3>

<ul>

<li>
Making power-sensitive hypotheses explicit in theory, analysis, and interpretation.<sup class="citation">2,3</sup>
</li>

<li>
Explicitly challenging and deconstructing the normalization and naturalization of inequality.<sup class="citation">2,3</sup>
</li>

</ul>

</div>

</div>

<details class="reference-block">

<summary>Notes</summary>

<ol>

 The tool provides selected examples of methodological challenges identified in the literature on quantitative intersectional analysis, together with approaches proposed to address them. These examples are derived from the literature analysis underlying the development of the tool. While not exhaustive, they illustrate how the framework can be applied to systematically reflect on methodological concerns. Readers are encouraged to use them as a starting point for further methodological discussion and for expanding the analytical toolkit of (quantitative) intersectional research. Although the examples presented here primarily focus on quantitative approaches, an analogous application of the framework to qualitative research may also prove fruitful. 
</li>

</ol>
